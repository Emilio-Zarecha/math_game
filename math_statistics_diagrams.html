One interactive diagram: the binomial probability mass function.

1 — Binomial Probability Mass Function

n independent trials, each a success with probability p. The bar at each i shows P(X = i). Bars from 0 up to k are shaded to show them accumulating into P(X ≤ k) — the area under the curve up to k. The red curve is the normal approximation N(np, np(1−p)).

P(X = i) = C(n, i) · pi · (1−p)(n−i)
