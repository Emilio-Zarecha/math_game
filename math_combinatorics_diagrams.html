Five interactive diagrams: counting trees, P vs C visualiser, Pascal's triangle, inclusion-exclusion, and stars and bars.

1 — Fundamental Counting Principle: Tree Diagram

Adjust the number of choices at each step. Every path from root to leaf is one complete outcome. The total equals the product of choices — the Fundamental Counting Principle.

2 — Permutations vs Combinations

Choose n items and select r of them. Toggle between ordered (permutation) and unordered (combination) to see why C(n,r) = P(n,r) ÷ r!.

3 — Pascal's Triangle

Click any cell C(n,k) to see its value and the two parent cells that sum to it. Use the buttons to highlight special patterns.

Click any cell to explore C(n, k)
Each interior cell equals the sum of the two cells directly above it — Pascal's Identity: C(n,k) = C(n−1,k−1) + C(n−1,k).
