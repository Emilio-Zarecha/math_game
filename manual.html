Player Manual — Chapter 1: Solving a Puzzle

Choosing a Puzzle

Use the two dropdowns at the top of the screen to pick a topic — Algebra, Arithmetic, Geometry, Trigonometry, or Calculus — and then select a specific problem from the list. Problems are labeled easy, medium, or hard. When you find one you want, press Load Puzzle. The question appears in the upper panel, and a diagram (for geometry, trigonometry, and calculus puzzles) is shown beneath it to give you a visual reference.

Reading the Step

Every puzzle is broken into a series of steps that walk you through the solution one move at a time. The current step is displayed in the large equation panel. Informational steps show a completed transformation — press → or Next → to continue when you are ready. Interactive steps contain one or more dashed-border drop zones inside the equation, waiting for you to fill them in. The step description below the equation tells you what the move is trying to accomplish.

Making a Move

The Palette on the left holds the pieces available for this step. Drag the correct piece from the palette and drop it onto the matching drop zone in the equation. If your choice is right the zone turns green; if it is wrong it flashes red and you can try again. Some steps require you to drop the same piece into more than one zone — the game will prompt you when another zone still needs to be filled. Once all drop zones in a step are satisfied the puzzle advances to the next step on its own.

Keyboard Navigation and the Animated Demo

You can move through the puzzle entirely with the keyboard. Every step — informational or interactive — waits for you before advancing. Press → to move forward: on an informational step it simply reveals the next transformation; on an interactive step it triggers the animated demo — the correct palette piece is highlighted, the target drop zone glows, and the piece flies into place over two seconds. Press ← at any time to retreat one step, cancelling any animation in progress. The Next → and ← Prev buttons behave identically to the arrow keys. Use Give Up This Step if you are stuck and want the answer revealed — this increments your hint count.

Finishing and Scoring

After the last step the completion screen appears. Press Final Answer at any point to jump straight there if you are confident you understand the solution. Your score bar at the top tracks puzzles solved and hints used across the session. Use Reset to restart the current puzzle from the beginning whenever you want more practice. To try a different problem, just pick a new one from the dropdowns and load it — your session totals carry over.
